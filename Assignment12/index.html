Augmentative and Alternative Communication

Augmentative and alternative communication (AAC) can support or replace spoken communication for individuals.

There are many types of AAC systems ranging from low-tech to high-tech devices. Some examples of low-tech systems are communication methods that do not require electronic devices, such as communication books/ boards, pictures/symbols, pen and paper, etc.

Low-Tech Devices

Low-tech devices are systems that do not need to rely on technology, such as pen/paper, communication boards, pictures/symbols, sign language, etc.

High-Tech Devices

High-tech devices rely on technology and can include eye gaze devices, speech generating devices, tablets, etc.
