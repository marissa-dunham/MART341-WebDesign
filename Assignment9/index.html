How to Ride a Washington State Ferry

Guide

Choose a Ferry Route

  • Mukilteo - Clinton
  • Edmonds - Kingston
  • Seattle - Bainbridge Island
  • Seattle - Bremerton
  • Fauntleroy - Vashon
  • Fauntleroy - Vashon
  • Southworth - Vashon
  • Pt Defiance - Tahlequah

Step 1

Check the real time ferry map to see the vessels in your area and plan your route. The Anacortes - San Juan Islands and Port Townsend - Coupeville routes allow vehicle reservations.

Step 2: Choose a Ferry Lane

Plan to arrive at the terminal at least 20 minutes in advance of the sailing. Peak traffic times include the morning eastbound from the islands and penninsulas, and the afternoon and evening westbound.

Step 3: Buy your Tickets

You may buy your tickets online or when you arrive.

  • Children and youth under 19 years of age travel free if they are not the driver of the vehicle.
  • Seniors and indivduals with disabilities may receive reduced fares but will need to have proof of eligibility.

Step 4: Board the Ferry when Instructed

Workers will help guide you to where you will park your vehicle.

Step 5: Park your Vehicle

Some tips if you decide to leave your vehicle:

  • Put on your emergency brake
  • Turn off your car alarm
  • Follow directions from loudspeakers
  • Workers will give instructions when unloading
