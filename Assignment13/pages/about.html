About

Olympic National Park is designated as a World Heritage Site and an International Biosphere Reserve by the United Nations. This park consists of high mountain peaks, lush rainforests, and sandy beaches. Some popular places inside the are: Hurricane Ridge, Elwha River Valley, Lake Crescent, and the Hoh Rain Forest.

The park is open 24 hours a day year round; however, some roads and campgrounds open seasonally.

It is incredibly important to read through the Leave No Trace Principles to help protect the park.

Seven Principles of Leave No Trace:

  1. Plan Ahead and Prepare
  2. Camp and Travel On Durable Surfaces
  3. Dispose of Waste Properly
  4. Leave What You Find
  5. Minimize Campfire Impacts
  6. Respect Wildlife
  7. Be Considerate of other Visitors
